Actress Amber Heard has been spared a conviction over a dog-smuggling saga, after she appeared in an Australian court Monday admitting she did not properly declare her two Yorkshire terriers when entering the country.

The illegal import of animals carries a maximum penalty of 10 years in prison and a fine of 102,000 Australian dollars ($75,000); the false document charge has a maximum penalty of a year in prison and a fine of 10,200 Australian dollars ($7,500).

The court magistrate gave Johnny Depp's wife a one-month good behavior bond sentence CNN-affiliate 7 News Australia said.
